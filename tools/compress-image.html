PNG files can't be meaningfully compressed in the browser.
PNG is a lossless format — the browser has no way to make it smaller without changing the format. To reduce file size, save as JPG or WebP instead:
JPG — best for photos, no transparency · WebP — smaller files, keeps transparency, supported by all modern browsers

Good to know

  • Everything happens in your browser — your image is never uploaded anywhere.
  • 82% quality is the sweet spot for JPG — files are typically 40–60% smaller with no visible difference.
  • PNG compression is less effective than JPG or WebP — consider converting to WebP for best results.
  • Use "by target size" if you need to fit an upload limit, like an email attachment or profile picture.
