Good to know

  • All analysis happens instantly in your browser — your text is never sent anywhere.
  • Reading time is estimated at 200 words per minute (average silent reading speed). Speaking time uses 130 words per minute (average presentation pace).
  • Sentences are counted by detecting . ! ? followed by a space or end of text. Numbers like "3.14" are not counted as sentence endings.
  • Paragraphs are blocks of text separated by a blank line.
  • The Flesch readability score rates text from 0–100. Higher scores mean easier reading. A score of 60–70 is considered standard prose (plain English). Below 30 is very difficult (legal/academic). The score works best for English text — it shows N/A for very short inputs or non-Latin text.
  • Sentence length — very long sentences (30+ words) are flagged to help you spot places where readability could improve.
  • ✦ Premium — keyword density: shows the top 10 most frequently used meaningful words in your text with their count and percentage. Common words like "the", "a", "is", "and" are automatically excluded so only content words appear.
